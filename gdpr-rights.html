Your GDPR Data Subject Rights

At domain, we are committed to protecting your personal data and ensuring you can exercise your rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable ePrivacy legislation. This page explains your rights clearly and how to exercise them.

Last Updated:

Overview

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) grants individuals specific rights regarding their personal data. As a data subject, you have the right to know what data we hold about you, how we use it, and to control how it is processed. We take these obligations seriously and have established clear procedures for handling all data subject requests.

All requests are handled in accordance with GDPR (EU) 2016/679 and the applicable national implementations, including ePrivacy Directive requirements. We aim to respond to all verified requests within 30 calendar days of receipt.

Your Rights Under GDPR

Below is a comprehensive overview of the rights you are entitled to exercise at any time as a resident of the European Union or a jurisdiction covered by GDPR.

Right of Access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (known as a Subject Access Request or SAR). This includes information about the purposes of processing, categories of data, recipients, and retention periods. We will provide this information free of charge within 30 days.

Right to Rectification

If any personal data we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request that we correct or complete it without undue delay. This ensures that the information we use for any processing activities remains accurate and up to date.

Right to Erasure

Also known as the "Right to Be Forgotten," you may request the deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected, you withdraw consent, or you object to processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds. Some legal exceptions may apply.

Right to Restriction of Processing

You can request that we restrict how we process your personal data in certain circumstances — for example, while verifying the accuracy of data you have contested, or when processing is unlawful but you prefer restriction over erasure. During restriction, we may only store your data, not use it.

Right to Data Portability

Where processing is based on your consent or a contract, and is carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit that data to another controller where technically feasible.

Right to Object

You have the right to object to processing of your personal data based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. If you object to direct marketing, we will cease processing your data for that purpose immediately, without exception.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we process your personal data based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to your withdrawal. Once you withdraw consent, we will cease processing your data for that specific purpose.

To withdraw consent, please contact us using the details provided in the Contact section below, or use the request form on this page.

How to Submit a GDPR Request

To exercise any of the rights listed above, please submit a request using one of the following methods. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request to ensure the security of your data.

By Email

Send your request to our Data Protection contact at: info@domain.com

Please include your full name, email address associated with your account, a description of your request, and any relevant reference numbers.

Via Request Form

Use the GDPR request form below on this page. Complete all required fields and specify the type of request you are submitting. We will acknowledge receipt within 5 business days.

Response Timeline

  • 1
    Acknowledgement: Within 5 business days of receiving your request, we will confirm receipt and inform you of next steps.
  • 2
    Identity Verification: We may request additional information to verify your identity. This time does not count against the 30-day response window.
  • 3
    Full Response: We will provide a full response within 30 calendar days of receipt of your verified request. In complex cases, we may extend this by up to two further months, with notification provided.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe that our processing of your personal data violates GDPR or your rights have not been adequately addressed, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant supervisory authority in your country of residence or the country where the alleged infringement took place.

For users in the European Union, the competent supervisory authority is the data protection authority in your member state. You can find a list of all EU supervisory authorities at the European Data Protection Board (EDPB) website.

We would, however, appreciate the opportunity to address your concerns before you approach any supervisory authority. Please contact us first so we can attempt to resolve the matter directly.
